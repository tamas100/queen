Brian May

Sir Brian Harold May CBE - born 19 July 1947

Brian is world renowned as Queen's lead guitarist, using the guitar he built from an old fireplace with his father whilst still at school, and is one of the world's most successful songwriters and producers. He has also collaborated with many other artists, including Cliff Richard, Meat Loaf, Diana Ross, Paul McCartney and Robbie Williams.

Early life

He was born in Hampton Hill. His mother was Scottish, his father was English. He studied Mathematics and Physics at Imperial College London, graduating with a BSc degree in Physics in 1968 with honours.

Following his graduation, May received a personal invitation from Sir Bernard Lovell to work at the Jodrell Bank Observatory while continuing to prepare his doctorate, but he declined.

Career outside the Queen

Early years

Brian formed his first band in 1964, titled '1984'. They split up in 1968, and several months later he formed 'Smile' with Roger Taylor and Tim Staffell. They lasted for two years before splitting up, when Tim left.

Solo career

In 1983, Brian went into the studios with four friends for a simple jamming session, and the result was released as a mini-album titled 'Starfleet Project'. 1992 saw the release of Brian's first proper solo album, 'Back To The Light'. 1998 saw the release of 'Another World', Brian's most recent studio album.
